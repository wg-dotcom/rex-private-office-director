Our Selection Rationale

Not an Assistant. The Person Who Keeps Everything Moving.

Rex isn't looking for someone who takes orders and follows up on tasks. Peter needs someone who thinks three steps ahead, who doesn't wait to be asked, and who can hold the complexity of a fast-moving private office without missing a beat. Discretion isn't a bullet point in the job description. It's the entire foundation.

So we filtered hard. We started by eliminating anyone with more than three employers in the last twelve years. No consultants. No freelancers. No one who needed a clear playbook handed to them. We were looking for candidates who've operated in high-trust, high-stakes environments — supporting C-suite principals, managing sensitive correspondence, running calendars that affect entire organizations.

Then we layered in the specifics: bilingual Spanish and English at professional fluency, US Visa confirmed, LATAM preferred, full EST time zone alignment, and a demonstrated ability to support someone's personal and professional life simultaneously without losing composure or confidentiality. We screened for career backgrounds rooted in consulting, finance, legal, and executive support at the principal level.

What we looked for in every conversation: These aren't people who describe their past roles as managing a process. They describe owning outcomes. They talk about what broke and how they fixed it before anyone noticed. They've sat in the room where real decisions happen, and they know how to be invisible when that's what's needed, and indispensable every day.
